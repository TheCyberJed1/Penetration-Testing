Our Testing Methodology

We follow a structured, repeatable methodology aligned with industry frameworks (PTES, OWASP, NIST SP 800-115) to ensure every engagement is thorough, defensible, and delivers real-world value.

1

Pre-Engagement & Scoping

Before any technical work begins, we work closely with your team to define the rules of engagement, confirm authorization, and set mutual expectations. This protects both parties and ensures testing doesn't disrupt critical operations.

  • Signed Statement of Work (SOW) and authorization documentation
  • Define in-scope and out-of-scope assets
  • Establish testing windows and emergency contacts
  • Threat-modeling session to prioritize testing focus
2

Reconnaissance & OSINT

We gather publicly available information about your organization the same way a real attacker would — without touching your systems yet.

  • Passive DNS enumeration and certificate transparency
  • OSINT for exposed credentials, leaked data, and employee information
  • Technology fingerprinting from public-facing assets
  • Shodan / Censys review of internet-exposed services
3

Scanning & Enumeration

We actively probe in-scope targets to map the attack surface, identify services, and discover potential entry points.

  • Port and service scanning (Nmap, Masscan)
  • Vulnerability scanning (authenticated and unauthenticated)
  • Web application crawling and directory enumeration
  • Active Directory enumeration (internal engagements)
4

Exploitation & Privilege Escalation

We attempt to exploit identified vulnerabilities to confirm exploitability and demonstrate real-world impact. We do this carefully — no production data is modified and DoS attacks are never executed.

  • Manual verification of critical and high findings
  • Privilege escalation attempts (local and domain)
  • Credential attacks (password spraying, hash cracking)
  • Lateral movement and persistence simulation
5

Post-Exploitation & Impact Analysis

We document what an attacker could realistically achieve from each compromised position, mapping technical findings to business impact.

  • Data access assessment (what could be stolen/modified?)
  • Attack path mapping and chaining of vulnerabilities
  • Business impact narrative for executive reporting
6

Reporting & Debrief

Every engagement concludes with a detailed written report and a live debrief call with your team to walk through findings, answer questions, and align on remediation priorities.

  • Executive summary written for non-technical leadership
  • Technical findings with CVSS scores and step-by-step reproduction steps
  • Prioritized remediation roadmap
  • Live debrief session included at no extra cost
7

Remediation Verification (Optional)

Within 30 days of the final report, we offer a complimentary re-test of all Critical and High findings to verify that your team's fixes are effective.

  • Targeted re-test of patched/mitigated findings
  • Updated report reflecting remediation status
  • Letter of attestation available upon request

Our Ethical Commitment

Every assessor signs a confidentiality agreement before beginning work.
