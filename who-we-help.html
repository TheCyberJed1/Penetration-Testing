Who We Help

We work best with organizations that take security seriously but don't yet have a full in-house red team. If you handle sensitive data, face regulatory requirements, or simply want to know where you're exposed — we're built for you.

🏥

Healthcare Organizations

Clinics, telehealth platforms, and healthcare SaaS companies handling PHI and navigating HIPAA compliance requirements.

  • HIPAA risk assessment requirements
  • Ransomware targeting patient data
  • Connected medical device security
  • Third-party vendor access risks
💳

Financial Services & Fintech

Banks, credit unions, payment processors, and fintech startups that process or store financial data and face PCI-DSS or SOC 2 requirements.

  • PCI-DSS scope reduction and validation
  • API security for payment integrations
  • SOC 2 Type II security controls testing
  • Fraud and account takeover risk
⚖️

Law Firms & Legal Services

Small to mid-size law firms protecting privileged client communications, case files, and settlement data from targeted attacks.

  • Highly targeted spear-phishing campaigns
  • Ransomware and business email compromise
  • Secure remote access for attorneys
  • Cloud storage misconfiguration risks
🛒

E-Commerce & Retail

Online retailers and brick-and-mortar shops with e-commerce platforms handling cardholder data and customer PII.

  • Web skimming / Magecart attack risks
  • PCI-DSS compliance gaps
  • Third-party plugin vulnerabilities
  • Supply chain compromises
🚀

Startups & SaaS Companies

Early-stage and growth-phase tech companies that need to demonstrate security posture to enterprise customers, investors, or compliance auditors.

  • Customer due diligence (DDQ) security questionnaires
  • Pre-launch web app security review
  • SOC 2 / ISO 27001 readiness testing
  • CI/CD pipeline and cloud security gaps
🏛

Nonprofits & Education

Mission-driven organizations and educational institutions with limited IT budgets that still handle sensitive donor, student, or community data.

  • Limited security budget and small IT teams
  • Ransomware and data theft targeting nonprofits
  • FERPA compliance for educational data
  • Grant or donor data protection requirements
"You don't have to be a Fortune 500 company to be a target — attackers go after whoever is most vulnerable, and smaller organizations with less mature security controls are often the easiest path."
— CyberJed Security, Client Advisory
